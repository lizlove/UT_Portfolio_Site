As an interaction designer and digital archivist, I am interested in innovative approaches to human-centered design and expanding access to cultural heritage resources through user-oriented digital collections.

In my current position at Harry Ransom Center I work in digital collections and public services - combining technical projects with an attention to user discovery. Previously, I was a teaching assistant for HCI and a UX researcher at Tk20, Inc.

Prior to graduate school, I was a member of the curatorial team for the 2008 Whitney Biennial and worked as an assistant curator at the Museum of Contemporary Art Santa Barbara.Â  I continue to organize exhibitions and produce scholarly writing.

I received my Master of Science in Information Studies from The University of Texas Austin in 2014. I also hold a BA with high honors in French and International Relations from University of California Santa Barbara and pursued coursework towards an MA in Art History at Hunter College.
