Comparing Archival Collections Using D3.js

Data Visualization | UT Austin

For a data visualization course I harnessed the D3 javascript library to build an interactive heat map that compares the growth and development of collections at the two largest UT archives over time. This map mined XML documents (coded EAD finding aids) from the Harry Ransom Center and the Briscoe Center for American History. Using Excel (Visual Basic) for data normalization/manipulation, I sorted the collections into Records(collections belonging to organizations), Papers(collections belonging to individuals or families), and Other(often thematic collections like Spanish comedias sueltas from the 18th century) to illustrate trends in collecting at the respective institutions.
