Journey to the Stewardship Game

National Digital Stewardship Fellows| New York, NY

Designed and programmed a four-level maze game using GameMaker software that teaches basics of born-digital preservation. Each level includes a threat to preservation embodied in a monster figure as well as a collection item to save. Successful completion of a level unlocks a preservation tool.

Crowdlearning Center Moodle Development

Information Evolution, Inc | Austin, TX

Quality control is one of the largest challenges facing users of crowd-sourced labor marketplaces like Amazon Mechanical Turk. In an earlier study, I determined that Mechanical Turk workers who had completed a Massive Online Open Course produced more accurate results. Expanding on these results, this capstone project features three online courses built to train and certify crowd-sourced workers for Austin-based data management firm, Information Evolution, Inc (IEI).

Previously, IEI relied on qualification tests to gauge worker skills. The new online courses and badges provide a customizable solution to the problem of qualified labor. Built using the Moodle open-source learning management system, the courses teach data refinement skills using a guided learning path of resources and activities. The custom courses enable IEI to train an agile workforce that produces high-quality output and can quickly adapt to changing market demands. Workers who completed the lessons and earned over 90% on the internal quizzes were awarded a badge built on the Mozilla Open Badges framework and registered through the accreditation service Cred.ly.
